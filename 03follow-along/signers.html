Most Famous Signers

Thomas Jefferson
The principal author of the Declaration, Jefferson is perhaps the most well-known signer. He went on to become the third President of the United States.
John Adams
A leading advocate for independence and a future second President of the United States, Adams was one of the most influential figures in pushing for the Declaration's adoption.
Benjamin Franklin
Renowned for his role as a diplomat, inventor, and philosopher, Franklin was a key figure in the American Revolution and was already an international figure by the time the Declaration was signed.
John Hancock
Known for his bold signature, Hancock was the president of the Continental Congress at the time. His name became synonymous with "signature" because of the size and prominence of his autograph.
Samuel Adams
A cousin of John Adams, Samuel was a prominent leader in the American Revolution and a founding member of the Sons of Liberty, a group that opposed British policies.
Richard Henry Lee
He was the one who proposed the resolution for independence in June 1776, setting the stage for the Declaration.
Robert Livingston
He was part of the committee that drafted the Declaration and helped guide its passage.
Roger Sherman
The only person to sign all four founding documents of the United States: the Continental Association, the Declaration of Independence, the Articles of Confederation, and the U.S. Constitution.
