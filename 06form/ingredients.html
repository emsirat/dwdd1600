Understanding Ingredients

What Do They Do?

Different ingredients support baked goods in different ways. Some are specific, like baking soda vs. baking powder. While others can be used as substitutions, like sour cream and Greek yogurt. Down below, we'll be going through a more in depth conversation on how ingredients play an important role in baking chemistry.

Ingredients and Their Roles

Flour
Provides structure (gluten = strength)
Sugar
Adds sweetness and moisture; helps browning.
Eggs
Bind ingredients, add moisture and richness, help with structure.
Butter/Oil
Adds fat for tenderness and flavor.
Leaveners (baking soda/powder, yeast)
Make baked goods rise.
Liquid (milk, water, juice)
Helps mix, activates gluten and leaveners.
Salt
Enhances the flavors and balances sweetness.

Substitutions Aren't Always Simple!

  1. Swapping out ingredients can effect a baked goods taste, texture, and how it rises.
  2. Experimenting with your ingredients is OKAY. But while you're first starting out, it's better to follow the recipes with the ingredients listed and only substitute if the baker has it listed as an option
